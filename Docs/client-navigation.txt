Licences and how to pay for a Squidly account

Once a licence is active, it will appear in the My Licences section below, or you can check your subscription at the Profile page.
Your licence can be managed or updated from this page at any time.

Editing & using an AAC board 
This video shows how to create, edit, and use an AAC board in Squidly.
From the dashboard, open the AAC Grid Editor. On the left, you’ll see a list of available topics. These are your AAC boards. You can search for an existing board or choose to copy, edit, or delete one
To create a new board, select the “+” button for New Topic. Start by giving your board a topic name, then choose a grid size that best suits the user’s needs. You can also choose whether to set the board to private or public.
Once the grid is created, you can customise each cell. Select a cell to edit its display value, which is the text shown on the button. 
You can add an icon by searching the icon library or uploading your own image or PDF.
Each cell can also be assigned a type, such as normal, noun, verb, adjective, or topic buttons, allowing boards to link together and support sentence building. Colours update automatically to reflect the selected type.
Topics can be linked to other boards. This makes it easy for users to move between boards during communication.
Once saved, your board appears in the topic list and can be searched at any time. Squidly also includes default AAC boards named “Voco Chat”. 
To use an AAC board during a live session, open the Access and select AAC. Search for the board you want, and it will appear instantly for the participant.
As the participant selects cells, words appear in the message bar and can be spoken aloud using the Speak button.

At the end of the quiz, a summary screen shows the participant’s results. You can also download a report after the session to review responses and progress.
Quizzes can be used flexibly during sessions, whether for learning, engagement, or assessment.

Using the eye-gaze / settings for eye-gaze (calibration / dwell time)
This video shows how to use Squidly’s webcam eye-gaze and adjust eye-gaze settings.
Go into settings and make sure the eye-gaze is toggled on.
In the session, open the Access from the toolbar. 
To begin using eye-gaze, select ‘Eye-Gaze’. If calibration is needed, choose Calibrate.
When calibration starts, the user is guided to position their face clearly within the camera view. The temperature bar on the right helps ensure the face is centred and visible before calibration begins. Try and fill-up the temperature bar.
Then press Calibrate to start calibration. Calibration points then appear on the screen. The user follows each point with their eyes as it moves through the calibration sequence.
Once calibration is complete, eye-gaze can be tested immediately within the session by pressing Test and interacting with the 9 point layout. Squidly will show your eye-gaze accuracy score. Typically an eye-gaze accuracy above 80% means the eye-gaze can be used effectively.
Calibration settings can be adjusted if needed.
Eye-gaze can be toggled on and off simultaneously both host and participant with easy access via the access button on the tool bar or pressing ‘e’ as a shortcut on the keyboard. 
To adjust eye-gaze and calibration settings for both host and participant, open Settings, then go to Access or Calibration. Here, you can fine-tune calibration settings, and dwell times.
You can also adjust the size of the calibration targets, calibration speed, and the type of visual guide used in calibration. 
Calibration and settings can be revisited at any time during the session. Particularly if head movement and a change in position has occurred at any point after calibration. 
Squidly sessions can also be interacted with using your personal eye-gaze device.

Sharing content / file 
In this video, we’ll show how to share content during a Squidly session.
Select Share in the toolbar.
To share your screen, select Screen. Your browser will prompt you to choose what you’d like to share: a browser tab, a window, or your entire screen. Once selected, your screen will be visible to everyone in the session.
To share a document or file, select File. You can upload items such as PDFs, documents, or images directly from your computer. 
When content is shared, accessible on-screen controls appear around the content.
These include zoom in, zoom out, reset, and navigation controls to move around the page which both host and participant can use together.
A grey border appears around shared content which tells you the aspect ratio of the participants screen and makes sure client’s have the best possible viewing experience of shared content.
Select Exit to stop sharing.
